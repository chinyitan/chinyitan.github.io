Research

Research #

I have done research in various fields of physics such as astrophysics, biophysics and particle physics in the last couple of years.

Modelling a strong gravitational lenses #

I am currently building an automated pipeline to model 160 strong gravitational lenses using their HST image. The model parameters obtained can be used to determine the inner slope of the dark matter profile in elliptical galaxies and aid in the measurement of the Hubble constant.

Probing the Properties of MACHOs with Ultra Faint Galaxies #

I had used the existence of a globular cluster in the dwarf galaxy Eridanus II to make theoretical constraints on dark matter. Using stochastic methods of tidal heating and the Superbox simulation, I estimated the lifespan of the cluster before it dissolves into the host galaxy and used it to obtain constraints on the abundance of Massive Compact Halo Objects (MACHOs).

Finding the structure-function mapping of polysaccharide degrading communities #

I am also currently creating models which can predict the metabolism of multiple-strain communities with different carbon sources using the respiration rate of a single bacteria strain. The respiration rate of the community is obtained through a custom-built setup that performs multiple pressure measurements in parallel.

Testing photomultiplier tubes for the WATCHMAN detector #

I participated in building a data acquisition system that can be used to test the efficiency of hundreds of photomultiplier tubes for use in an anti-neutrino detector.

Testing water-based scintillators for future neutrino detectors #

I investigated the optical properties and stability of Linear Alkylbenzene Sulfonate to determine its suitability to be used in future neutrino detectors.
